## Users

Kreis is for university students, currently centered on UADE, who want a fast mobile way to discover campus events, join interest communities, and participate in short community conversations. They are often checking the app between classes, while planning the week, or while deciding what to do around campus with other students.

## Product Purpose

Kreis exists to make campus life easier to enter. The product should help students find events, communities, social plans, workshops, and casual groups without feeling like a corporate events directory. Success means the first screen quickly communicates what is happening next, where to tap, and which community spaces feel alive.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, warm, local, and student-centered. Kreis should feel optimistic and social, with a confident orange identity and a friendly visual language that still stays practical for repeated mobile use. The app can have expressive brand moments, but the core product screens should stay readable and direct.

## Anti-references

Avoid cold blue-gray social feed clones, generic SaaS dashboards, corporate event-listing portals, overly glossy loader effects, long splash screens that feel like ads, heavy glassmorphism used as decoration, and motion that blocks fast access to the app.

## Design Principles

- Mobile-first and thumb-friendly: the app should feel native to a student checking it on a phone.
- Start with what matters now: events, communities, profile, and creation flows should be obvious.
- Warm, not corporate: product surfaces should feel social and local without becoming noisy.
- Brand moments can be bold: saturated orange belongs in launch and identity moments.
- Motion should invite, then get out of the way: use brief transitions that respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible mobile interactions with clear hit areas, readable text, visible focus states, sufficient contrast across light and dark modes, and support for `prefers-reduced-motion`. Avoid relying on color alone for meaning, especially for active states, official badges, and filters.
