# Kreis Product Context

Kreis is a React + Vite prototype for a mobile-first university social app. It helps students discover campus events, join interest communities, and publish short posts inside those communities.

## Users

Primary users are university students who want a fast way to find plans, groups, workshops, and casual social spaces around campus life. The current mock data is centered on UADE events and student communities across design, technology, gaming, culture, sport, business, music, volunteering, and social plans.

## Product Purpose

Kreis should feel like a warm connector between students, not a corporate events directory. The interface should make it obvious where to enter: events, communities, profile, and creation flows. The app should feel lightweight, optimistic, and social, while still being direct enough for repeated mobile use.

## Brand And Tone

Kreis uses a confident orange identity with cream surfaces, warm shadows, and green accents. The tone is energetic, friendly, local, and student-centered. Motion should feel alive and welcoming, but it should not delay access to the product.

## Design Principles

- Mobile-first and thumb-friendly.
- Brand moments can be saturated orange, especially app launch and identity surfaces.
- Product screens should stay readable, calm, and task-oriented.
- Motion should convey entry, connection, and readiness.
- Keep transitions brief and respectful of reduced-motion preferences.

## Anti-References

- Generic social feed clones with cold blue-gray UI.
- Long splash screens that feel like ads.
- Overly glossy startup-loader effects.
- Heavy motion that blocks the user from quickly reaching the app.
